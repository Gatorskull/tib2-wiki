# Open World Farming

Open-world farming is repeatable PvE: killing NPCs, harvesting asteroids and debris, progressing missions, and collecting loot while managing travel risk.

## Core farming loop

1. Pick a map you can survive in.
2. Kill NPCs for XP, loot, mission progress, and pity loot.
3. Harvest asteroids, debris, and resource nodes.
4. Scrap low-value items or resources for [Credits](../../../Entities/Currencies/Credits.md), or sell useful gear to players.
5. Repair ships and equipment before durability becomes a problem.

## Risk and reward

| Area | Tradeoff |
|------|----------|
| Human-controlled PvE maps | Safer, lower risk, good for leveling and low-stress farming. |
| PvP maps | Higher risk, but better XP/loot incentives and faster [Pity Loot](../../../Items/Pity-Loot.md) progress. |
| Higher Security Rating maps | Stronger enemies and better reward potential. See [Security Rating](../../../World/Maps/Security Rating.md) for known level bands and missing live-data fields. |

## Harvesting

[Harvester drones](../../../Items/Drones.md) gather resources from asteroids and debris. Most general-purpose farming ships want at least one Harvester unless they are built for a focused combat role.

Useful harvesting notes:

- Bonuses that increase resources harvested improve harvesting results.
- The **Drone Master** special can reduce harvesting cooldown.
- The **Freighter** ship mod improves harvesting, but reduces damage.

!!! warning "Needs data"
    The in-game wiki says Freighter doubles harvesting chances, while gootchat describes the ship's harvests yielding 2x normal amount. Confirm the exact current behavior before writing a precise formula.

## Economy reference

Market values change. The wiki should avoid fixed current prices unless they are dated observations. Use the [Credits](../../../Entities/Currencies/Credits.md) page for stable currency rules and player advice for live pricing.
