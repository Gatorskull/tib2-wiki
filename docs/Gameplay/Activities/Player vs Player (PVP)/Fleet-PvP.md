# Fleet PvP Rules

Fleet PvP is open-world player combat organized around drivers, assist leaders, collision limits, and shared target sorting.

For community tactics, see [Fleet PvP Strategy](../../../Strategy/Fleet-PvP.md). For cattabliss' authored guide material, see [Fleet PvP Quick Start](../../../Getting-Started/Quick-Start-Guides/Fleet-PvP-Quick-Start.md).

## Drivers and assist leaders

A **driver** is the ship a fleet follows. Fleet calls often link drivers in chat as **D1**, **D2**, **D3**, or similar labels.

Use **My Assist Leader** on a linked driver ship to:

- Bring that ship to the top of your allied ship list while it is in sector.
- Mark it with a yellow square on the map.
- Make it easier to jump to the fleet and follow the correct ship.

If a driver dies or gets too far away, players may need to refollow another driver. Follow distance rules live on [Sectors](../../../World/Maps/Sectors.md#following-ships).

## Collision

Fleet stacks are subject to [collision](../../../World/Maps/Sectors.md#collision-stacking-limit). Local sector ship counts can be enabled in settings; see [Recommended Settings](../../../Getting-Started/Settings.md).

## Sort calls and targeting UI

Enemy sort orders make allied target lists display in the same order.

The mechanics page should only document that sort controls exist and why fleets use shared sort calls. Specific sort-call choices and driver-building advice belong in [Fleet PvP Strategy](../../../Strategy/Fleet-PvP.md).

## Interdictors

[Interdictors](../../../Ships/Interdictor-Carrier.md) can slow enemy movement and targeting in affected sectors. Move-speed bonuses can offset Interdictor movement penalties, and [Gravity Drives](../../../Items/Gravity-Drives.md) negate the movement penalty for ships that equip them.
