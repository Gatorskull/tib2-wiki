# Carrier Builds

Carrier build advice changes with enemy Interdictors, available engines, corporation support, and fleet habits. Treat this page as community guidance, not game rules.

For stable mechanics, see [Carrier Leadership](../Ships/Carrier-Leadership.md), [Size and Targeting](../Ships/Size-And-Targeting.md), [Interdictor Carrier](../Ships/Interdictor-Carrier.md), and [Gravity Drives](../Items/Gravity-Drives.md).

## What Carriers Do

Carriers can anchor fleet movement through Carrier Leadership while the player is in a corporation. That makes them important for moving slower ships together.

Carrier Leadership does not remove the need to understand movement caps, warm-up times, following range, or Interdictor penalties.

## Common Build Goals

Carrier builds often balance:

- Movement speed and movement penalty resistance.
- Enough durability to stay alive while acting as the fleet anchor.
- Equipment choices that support the fleet's purpose.
- Gravity Drive or other movement tools when the build is focused on travel.

Exact item choices are community/meta guidance. Keep item stat formulas on [Item Reference](../Items/Item-Reference.md) and [Stats](../Items/Stats.md).

## Interdictor Pressure

Enemy Interdictors increase movement and targeting time in affected sectors. Extra movement speed can still matter even when a ship is at its size-based minimum move time because penalties can push movement back above that minimum.

[Gravity Drives](../Items/Gravity-Drives.md) negate Interdictor movement penalties for equipped ships.
