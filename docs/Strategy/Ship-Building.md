# Ship Building Strategy

Ship building advice changes with available ships, corporation shipyards, market supply, and balance patches. Treat this page as community guidance, not game rules.

For stable mechanics, start with [Ships](../Ships/index.md), [Ship Reference](../Ships/Ship-Reference.md), [Ship Crafting](../Ships/Ship-Crafting.md), [Equip Points](../Ships/Equip-Points.md), and [Equipment Slots](../Items/Equipment-Slots.md).

## Choosing A First Crafted Ship

Good first ships depend on what your corporation can build and what role you need. Before committing resources, check:

- Required player level and corporation membership time.
- Skull and Relic cost.
- Shipyard faction and level.
- Base Equip Points and slot layout.
- Whether the ship fills a farming, Arena, support, or PvP role.

Use [Ship Reference](../Ships/Ship-Reference.md) for requirements and [Faction Ship Notes](Faction-Ship-Notes.md) for community faction notes.

## Building Around Gear

Most builds are constrained by [Equip Points](../Ships/Equip-Points.md), item rank, and slot behavior.

- Weapons, armor, shields, and storage can change Equip Point pressure through rank.
- Weapons, drones, and batteries use first-slot versus auxiliary-slot rules.
- Technology from auxiliary items applies in full, but item stats are reduced.
- Diminishing returns make heavy stat stacking less efficient.

For item rules, use [Item Reference](../Items/Item-Reference.md), [Item Upgrades](../Items/Item-Upgrades.md), and [Diminishing Returns](../Items/Diminishing-Returns.md).
