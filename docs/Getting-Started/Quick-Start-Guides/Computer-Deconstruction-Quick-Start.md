# Computer Deconstruction Quick Start

*By cattabliss.*

**Deconstructing CPUs (computers)** You will notice some players will give advice not to scrap CPUs (computers, cabal MK, havok MK, toil MK, sage MK, warrior MK, etc). The reason for this is because when 1,000 points of computers of the same type are deconstructed, the game will grant you a valuable item that can upgrade a Tech 1 computer to a Tech 2 computer, and even a Tech 2 computer to a Tech 3 computer. Tech computers are similar to MK computers, but grant + equip point bonuses and higher positive stats than their MK counterparts. There are three ranks of tech computers, Tech I, Tech II and Tech III, with the higher numbers granting additional EP points and higher stat buffs. Initially, you might think, why not deconstruct every CPU to generate these kits? There's a good reason why. A few things to note:

1. Every computer deconstructed will grant 1 point (towards 1,000) for every rarity rank towards that class of computer, in addition to an equal number of points towards a victor computer. So, a common computer grants 1 point, an uncommon grants 2 points, a rare grants 3, and so on.
2. The first FOUR times you hit 1,000 points of any category, you will receive a kit that will upgrade a computer from Tech 1 to Tech 2. Every FIFTH time you hit 1,000 points of any category, you will be given the initial Tech 1 to 2 kit, PLUS a kit that increases Tech 2 to Tech 3.
3. The key to all this is to ensure that the FIFTH unlock is of a computer type that you desire. It sounds easier said than done. That's because, every time you deconstruct a CPU, you will also add points to the Victor-type computer, which most players will agree is the least useful. You want to avoid inadvertently unlocking a victor.
4. You can check your progress in CPU deconstruction points by clicking your player name > crafting > blueprint unlock progress. The information is at the very top.

Deconstructing computers require significant planning for one simple reason. Every time you deconstruct ANY CPU, you will add points to the class of CPU you deconstructed, in addition to points to the "Victor" class. This means that if you deconstruct EVERY computer, the Victor class will unlock at a rate of up to 6 times faster than any other computer, potentially spoiling the timing of your desired unlock.

To help mitigate the impact of the victor unlocks, many players will often deconstruct their desired CPU until they are around 20 to 50 points away from 1,000. At that point, they will deconstruct other computers until their FOURTH kit is unlocked. Then, they will finish the final 20 to 50 points of their desired computer for the FIFTH. Since it is extremely important to ensure every FIFTH (so, 5, 10, 15, 20, etc) unlock is of a desired computer, you should also regularly check which unlock you have already reached. You can do this in the player stats menu. Click your name > click stats at the bottom, then scroll down.
