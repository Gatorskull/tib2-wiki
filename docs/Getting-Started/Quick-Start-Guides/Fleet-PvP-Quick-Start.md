# Fleet PvP Quick Start

*By cattabliss.*

**Joining PVP -- fleet vs fleet** Fleet versus fleet PVP often happens spontaneously, or when fleet commanders call for players to stage in alliance chat. Typically, a call will consist of a commander/leader (highlighted chat) posting a link to a structure, and a link to a ship. Your job when joining is to ensure you follow a few rules that will help ensure a good experience for everyone on your team.

1. **Turn on your sector ship counts.** There is a maximum of 75 allied ships permitted in a single sector, any higher and every ship in the fleet will lose HP until enough ships die to get back to under 75. Do not be the player to make the stack exceed 75. To turn on sector ship counts, visit the three line menu (top left) > settings > gameplay > gameplay settings > display local sector ship counts. Make sure this is checked off in green.
2. **Set assist lead on up to three drivers.** Fleet vs fleet only happens with drivers. These are the ships being linked in alliance chat when a call for PVP is issued. Typically, drivers will list themselves by number: D1, D2, D3, to represent drivers 1, 2 and 3 respectively. Click each ship link, and to the right, click "my assist leader". This will highlight the ship on the map in a yellow square, and also bring the ship on top of any stack of ships you are present in, allowing you to easily follow this ship or find it on the map.
3. **Follow sort orders** During PVP, there is a lot going on and the primary method most players communicate by, including commanders issuing orders, is alliance chat. Pay attention to sort calls. Common sort calls can include:

- small hulls
- fastest movement
- largest size
- most in danger
- worst gear

... and so on. Selecting the sort that is called ensures that the order you see enemy ships in is the same order your alliance mates see them. It helps us all shoot the same target. To select your sort, click the three line menu (top left) > sort enemy > click the called sort.

## How do I make sure my ships are geared for PVP?

Fleet PVP is not the same as arena PVP, but there are ways to adjust your gear to ensure you get skulls and stay alive to do so. First, ensure you have at least 10,000 hull. The most common sort is "smallest hull" and newer players tend to get targeted first, and eliminiated quickly. By having a sufficient hull point pool, you can mitigate this.

The next most important stat is targeting speed. As long as you are able to hit an enemy before it dies, you will receive skulls for the kill. There are times when you target an enemy ship, and the ship dies before you can fire a shot. This mean you won't get skulls for the kill. The solution for this is targeting speed, which can be adjusted by items such as moltic batteries, graviton/matter reverter/helical railgun, arsenal storages, and drone master specials, or through void items with a ^ modifier for targeting speed. The max targeting speed bonus is 65%, reaching this or exceeding it (up to 95% to neutralize enemy interdictor debuffs) will help you achieve kills faster and earn more skulls.
